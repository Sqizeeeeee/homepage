University of Cambridge:

Hinc lucem et pocula sacra

The University of Cambridge, founded in 1209, is one of the oldest and most prestigious universities in the world. It was established by scholars and students who left Oxford following conflicts with the local townspeople. Cambridge quickly developed its own distinctive system of education and collegiate structure, with the first college, Peterhouse, founded in 1284.

Five main academic fields at Cambridge are:

  • Sciences (including Physics, Chemistry, Biology)
  • Engineering and Technology
  • Humanities
  • Social Sciences
  • Mathematics

Cambridge has a remarkable history of famous alumni including Isaac Newton, Charles Darwin, Stephen Hawking, and Alan Turing, among many others. Over 100 Nobel Prize winners have been associated with Cambridge, highlighting its profound impact on science, technology, and humanities.

Like Oxford, Cambridge remains a leader in research and education with a vibrant academic community and strong traditions that continue to influence global higher education. Its unique collegiate system and rigorous academic programs make it a top choice for students worldwide.
